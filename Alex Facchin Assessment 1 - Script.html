My interest in IT gradually grew on me from when I was around 5 years old. When I was 5 years old I spent time in Tokyo, Japan whilst my parents had jobs. Video games and consoles aside, Tokyo at the time had really embraced technology. Moving into today’s world, I’d consider technology to be an imperative part of my life. It encompasses everything from communicating with friends and family through my phone, completing work remotely from my personal equipment at home to using online collaboration tools online for work as well.

Personality Profiles

Myers Briggs Type Indicator

ISTJ - The Inspector

ISTJ'S are responsible organisers, driven to create and enforce order within systems and institutions. they are neat and orderly, inside and out, and tend to have a procedure for everything they do.

ISTP - The Craftsperson

ISTP'S are observant artisans with an understanding of mechanics and an interest in troubleshooting. They approach their environments with a flexible logic, looking for practical solutions to the problems at hand.

ESTJ - The Supervisor

ESTJ's are hardworking traditionalists, eager to take charge i organising projects and people. Oderly, rule-abiding, and conscientious. ESTK's like to get things done, and tend to go about projects in a systematic, methodical way.

Learning Style

Learning Preferences

Pragmatist Style

Pragmatists need to be able to see how to put the learning into practice in the real world. Abstract concepts and games are of the limited use unless they can see a way to put the ideas into action. Pramatists are likely to be experimenters, trying out new ideas, theories and techniques to see if they work.

Activits Style

Activists like to iummerse themselves fully in new experiences. they enjoy the 'here and now', and tend to be open minded, enthusiastic and flexible. They have a tendency to act first and consider the consequences later, and they often seek to centre activities around themselves.

Reflector Style

Reflectors prefer to stand back and observe. They tend to be cautious, preferring to take a back seat. They like to collect and analyse information to help them reach their conclusions, which they may take considerable time and effort to develop. Reflectors see the big picture by using information gathered from previous experience as well as the here and now.

Theorist Style

Theorists like to think through problems in a logical manner. They value rationality and objectivity, and like to assimilate disparate facts into coherent theories. They are disciplined, aiming to fit things into a rational oder.

Interactive Leadership Style Assessment by University of Southern California

Interactive Leadership Style

What do these test results mean for myself?

As a third test, I took the Interactive Leadership Style Assessment made by the University of Southern California. I believe this test helps myself become self-aware of my personal traits and possible clashes that could arise when forming a group. The result I achieved was ‘Transformative Leader’. According to the test, Transformational Leaders foster curiosity in their teams and work as agents of change. They work together to improve conditions and achieve goals. Inspiration and cooperation drive transformational leaders towards positive change.

How do I think these results may influence your behaviour in a team?

I believe the results may have varying influence on my behaviour in a team. Personally, I believe I adapt according to the personalities that are in the group, except for if there is an authoritarian figure in the group. I tend to adapt really well into groups especially if there are clearly defined roles within the group.

How should I take this into account when forming a team?

First and foremost I do think it is important to be self-aware and aware of other individuals personality traits when it comes to forming a team. The ideal situation would be maximise cohesion within the team and minimise personality conflicts in order to achieve the desired end result. Using the results from this test, it would be imperative to take into account other’s leadership styles and identify characteristics that I lack that another individual may have a strong attributes in.
