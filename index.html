Interest in IT

Where my interest in IT began

My interest in IT had sparked at roughly the age of 5. At that time in my life, I spent time in Tokyo, Japan whilst my parents worked. During the time of living in Tokyo, there was an obvious language language barrier so a great way of bonding with other children my age was through video games. At the time, there was the release of the first Sony PlayStation. Other gaming consoles from Nintendo and Sega were quite common and had been established in the industry. Video games and consoles aside, Tokyo at the time really embraced technology. I have few memories from that time in my life, but I do remember robotics in the house starting to emerge into the market. Moving into today’s world, I’d consider technology to be an imperative part of my life. In my life, it encompasses various forms of social media and other communication applications through my phone, completing work remotely from personal and work equipment at home to using collaboration tools online for university and work.

Why I chose RMIT

At the point of leaving High School, I did have a passion for Architecture. I decided to go that route for the foreseeable future. Moving forward in my professional career, I don’t see there to be a continual passion for Architecture or Engineering. After doing some light browsing to further my studies, I came across Open Universities Australia and looked into completing an IT degree. From the available degree’s to complete online, RMIT provides a wide range of subjects and specific topics of interest along the way. A nother major factor in deciding to go the online route was flexibility. With working full time, studying online allows me to study at my own pace and to comfortably set my own schedule on how many subjects I take on at once.

Learning Style

Learning Preferences

What is David Kolb's Learning Styles and Experimental Learning Cycle?

David Kolb is an American Educational Theorist who developed a quiz to develop and identify my personal preference in learning.

Pragmatist Style

Pragmatist need to be able to see how you put the learning into practice in the real world. Abstract concepts and games are of the limited use unless they can see a way to put the ideas into action. Pragmatists are likely to be experimenters, trying out new ideas, theories and techniques to see if they work.

Activits Style

Activists like to immerse themselves fully in new experiences. They enjoy the ‘here and now’, and tend to be open minded, enthusiastic and flexible. They have a tendency to act first and consider the consequences later, and they often see to center activities around themselves.

Reflector Style

Reflectors prefer to stand back and observe. They tend to be cautious, preferring to take a back seat. They like to collect and analyse information to help them reach their conclusions, which they may take considerable time and effort to develop. Reflectors see the big picture by using information gathered from previous experience as well as the here and now.

Theorist Style

Theorists like to think through problems in a logical manner. They value rationality and objectivity, and like to assimilate disparate facts into coherent theories. They are disciplined, aiming to fit things into a rational order.
